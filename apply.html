What this is

Most phone sales jobs put you on a base salary, hand you a quota you don't control, and cap what you can earn. This isn't that.

BuildSignals books appointments for home remodeling contractors across the US, and we're looking for people who are hungry enough to turn real effort into real income. Our average setter earns $4,000 a month — commission only, no cap, working remote on a proven script we hand you from day one.

We promote from within. Every closer on our team started exactly where you'd be starting.

What you'll actually do

Your day is calls. You're working a list of homeowner leads, running our script, and booking qualified homeowners onto a contractor's calendar for a free estimate. That's the job.

It's a volume game — the more calls you make, the more appointments you set, and the more you get paid. No fluff, no busywork. Dial, talk, book, repeat.

$750Per close

How you get paid

You're commission-only to start, with no cap on what you can make. Set an appointment, and once it closes into a signed job, you get $750 for it. No close, no commission on that one — so how well you set the appointment actually matters.

Where this goes

This role is a launchpad, not a landing spot. Once you've proven you can set appointments that close, you move into the closer seat — running the actual sales calls instead of just booking them.

Closers come onto our payroll, and our average closer earns $112,000 a year. For the right person, that jump can happen in months, not years — which is exactly why we need people who show up hungry and put in the reps.

You're a fit if

  • You're hungry You want more than a paycheck, and you're willing to put in the work to get it.
  • You can take a no Most calls end in rejection. That doesn't slow you down — you shake it off and dial the next number.

  • You're a talker People like talking to you. You're warm, easy on the phone, and confident with strangers.
  • Background helps Time in contracting or home services helps. Previous sales experience helps more. Neither is required, but both move you to the front of the line.
  • You're in the US Must be based in the United States with strong spoken English.
